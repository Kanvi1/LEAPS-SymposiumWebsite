So what is this symposium all about, you ask?

Resiliency

re·​sil·​ien·​cy

  • Resilience refers to the capacity to adapt and recover from disruptions, ensuring the maintenance or restoration of functionality.
  • Resilient infrastructure enables communities to endure and manage adverse conditions, such as power outages or natural disasters, maintaining essential services even in challenging situations.
  • Resilient design involves creating structures and communities that can withstand extreme weather and external threats. It emphasizes practical and realistic solutions to mitigate the impact of such challenges
